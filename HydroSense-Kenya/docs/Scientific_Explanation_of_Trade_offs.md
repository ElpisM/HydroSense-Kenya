## Three Strategies Compared

| Strategy | Total Water | Mean S | Min S | Stress Days | Cost |
|----------|------------|--------|-------|-------------|------|
| Conservative (5mm/day) | 150 mm | 28.5% | 26.1% | 0 | High |
| **Optimal (responsive)** | **82 mm** | **27.2%** | **24.5%** | **2–3** | **Moderate** |
| Minimal (no irrigation) | 0 mm | 20.1% | 15.2% | 15+ | Very low |

## Core Trade-off

More water → higher moisture → lower stress → higher cost
Optimal point: **45% water reduction for only 2–3 marginal stress days**

## Yield Impact by Moisture

| Moisture Range | Yield Potential | Status |

| S > 35% | 100% | Optimal |

**Strategy yields:** Conservative=100%, Optimal=98–99%, Minimal=30–50%

## Economic Analysis

| Item | Conservative | Optimal | Minimal |
|------|-------------|---------|---------|
| Water pumped | 150 mm | 82 mm | 0 mm |
| Pumping days | 30 | ~8 | 0 |
| Relative cost | 100% | ~50% | 0% |
| Crop viability | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ |

## Optimal Trade-off Point
- **Water:** 82mm (45% reduction vs conservative)
- **Yield loss:** <2%
- **Cost reduction:** ~50%
- **Risk:** Acceptable (crops tolerate 2–3 brief stress days)

## Decision Rules
```
S < 25%  → Irrigate 6–8mm
S > 35%  → Skip
7-day forecast rain > 20mm → Skip
Visible stress → Emergency irrigation
```

## When to Use Each Strategy

| Strategy | Appropriate When |
|----------|-----------------|
| Conservative | High-value crops, abundant water, minimal monitoring capacity |
| **Optimal** | **Commercial farm, monitoring capability, cost matters** |
| Minimal | Never for farming (research/fallow only) |

## Conclusion
Optimal irrigation is sustainable economically, environmentally, and socially. Water conservation and crop health are not competing — intelligent responsive management achieves both.
